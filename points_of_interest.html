Points of Interest A Guided Tour

Ebola Detail View - March 24 2014

Guinea has just announced that the mystery hemmorhagic fever that has taken 59 lives is indeed Ebola. The Shewhart chart for the day shows an enormous spike, and the map plot clearly shows the concentration of media activity around western Africa.

MERS Detail View - April 14 2014

Note the high confidence in the news articles that detail the spread of the virus in the Middle East. In early May, the CDC would confirm the first two cases to arrive in the USA, both in travelers who had visited Saudi Arabia.

Ebola / Influenza Comparison View - October 7 2015

What would you do? If you were an American NGO analyst, would you send your volunteers to Africa at greater cost to fight a deadlier disease, or would you mobilize them at home in the USA to help fight the flu since it is more contagious? Which disease does the classifier demonstrate higher confidence in as a potential outbreak?

Ebola Detail View - July 31 2014

The full-blown outbreak is under way. The WHO declared a public health emergency 8 days later. At this point, the death toll was 729. More than 10000 people would die during the year to follow.

Mumps Detail View - August 4 2015

Not all outbreaks are wide-spread and fatal. The mumps outbreak that began on a University of Illinois campus ultimately grew to more than 80 confirmed cases. Mumps is highly contagious, especially when people live in close quarters such as dorm rooms.
